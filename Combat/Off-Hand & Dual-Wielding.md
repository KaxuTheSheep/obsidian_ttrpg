### Off-Hand Strikes
If you are wielding a **Light** weapon in your off-hand:
- When you make a **melee [[Attacks|Attack]]** with your main-hand weapon, you may make **one Off-Hand Strike** as part of the **same Full [[Actions|Action]]**.
- The **Off-Hand Strike** uses **only the weapon’s [[Damage Types|Damage]] Die** (no [[Attributes|Attribute]]).
- The **Off-Hand Strike resolves at**:
	**Final [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] = Off-Hand Weapon Base [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] + [[Attributes|Attribute]] – 2**
This represents the slight inefficiency of coordinating two [[weapons]] **without making it weak**.
#### Defensive Benefit
If you are wielding **two [[weapons]]** and declare **[[Parry]]**, you gain:
- **+1 to [[Parry]] Tests**
_(This stacks with Guarded and other modifiers.)_
#### Restrictions
- The off-hand weapon must be **Light**.
- Only **one** Off-Hand Strike per **[[Rounds|Round]]**, regardless of how many [[Attacks]] you take.

#### Buckler Compatibility
Bucklers can be strapped to the forearm, allowing use alongside Two-Handed, Versatile, and Dual Wield loadouts without penalty to either the Buckler or the weapon.

#### [[Actions|Action]] Fatigue
Off-Hand Strikes do not trigger or accumulate [[Actions|Action]] Fatigue independently. They share the fatigue of the main hand [[Attacks|attack]] and are not tracked separately.

#### Versatile [[Weapons]]
A Versatile weapon wielded alongside an off-hand weapon is considered One-Handed and does not gain the [[Damage Types|damage]] die step. Both hands must be free to grip the weapon for the Two-Handed bonus to apply.
