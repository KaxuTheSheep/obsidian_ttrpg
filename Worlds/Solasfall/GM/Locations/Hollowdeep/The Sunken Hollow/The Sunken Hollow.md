### Overview
The Sunken Hollow is a natural cave system serving as one of the entry nexuses accessible from [[The Shattered Expanse]]. Unlike the Expanse's mirrored, disorienting geometry, the Hollow is a straightforward — if alien — stretch of natural rock: stone passages, underground water, and chambers lit by faintly bioluminescent moss and fungi growing along the walls. It is intended as a more accessible first proper destination for explorers, teaching the fundamentals of [[Hollowdeep]] survival without the perceptual hostility of deeper or stranger nexuses.

The strangeness here is restrained. Things mostly look like things should. They just aren't quite right.

### Structure
**[[Solasfall]] [[Rift]] Chamber**  
_Connects to: Tunnel A, Tunnel B, Tunnel C_  
The natural entry point and the calmest space in the Hollow. Bioluminescent moss and fungi grow thickest here, casting a steady pale-green glow across old Explorers' Guild trail markers and the remains of a previous expedition's camp — cold firepits, a collapsed tent frame, nothing recent. Aphids graze undisturbed near the walls; ants pass through occasionally on foraging routes but rarely linger.

**Tunnel A**  
_Connects to: [[Solasfall]] [[Rift]] Chamber ↔ The Ford_  
A plain connecting passage, damp underfoot as it nears the Ford. No fauna of note beyond stray aphids.

**The Ford**  
_Connects to: Tunnel A ↔ Main Hall_  
A shallow underground stream cuts across the passage here, forming a natural ford. Water reflects the bioluminescence in shifting ripples. Cockroaches scavenge along the banks, retreating into cracks in the streambed when disturbed rather than fighting.

**Tunnel B**  
_Connects to: [[Solasfall]] [[Rift]] Chamber ↔ Stalactite Passage_  
Dry and unremarkable, rising slightly as it approaches the Stalactite Passage.

**Stalactite Passage**  
_Connects to: Tunnel B ↔ Main Hall, Narrow Vent (down)_  
A drier chamber dense with heavy stalactite and stalagmite formations, some fused into columns. Ant Workers move constantly along the walls and ceiling here, tending aphid herds nested in the mineral folds. A narrow vent in the chamber floor, easy to miss without deliberate searching, drops toward the Lower Level.

**Tunnel C**  
_Connects to: [[Solasfall]] [[Rift]] Chamber ↔ The Squeeze_  
Gradually narrows as it progresses, giving early warning of the tight space ahead.

**The Squeeze**  
_Connects to: Tunnel C ↔ Deep Tunnel D_  
A low-ceilinged, tight passage requiring a stoop or crawl in places. Poor sightlines and dense shadow make it ideal ambush ground — a Mantis favours this space, relying on its camouflage against the restless light. Cockroaches fleeing the Ford or Main Hall often retreat here, squeezing through gaps too small for pursuit.

**Main Hall**  
_Connects to: The Ford, Stalactite Passage, Deep Tunnel E, Tarantula Burrow_  
The nexus's primary setpiece — a high-ceilinged chamber with multiple entry points and a significant ledge drop offering genuine tactical elevation. Bioluminescent growth is strongest here after the [[Rift]] Chamber, giving good visibility for the space's size. Ant Soldiers may muster here in response to colony distress signalled from Stalactite Passage or the Ford.

**Tarantula Burrow**  
_Connects to: Main Hall only (dead end)_  
A single concealed burrow off the Main Hall, webbed at the entrance and littered with old prey remains — carapace fragments, ant husks, the odd Guild marker chewed beyond recognition. The Tarantula's lair. Not a through-route; a deliberate confrontation space.

**Deep Tunnel D**  
_Connects to: The Squeeze ↔ Transitional Passage, Shattered Expanse Portal Alcove_  
Narrow and dim, with little to no natural bioluminescence — deliberately harder to navigate than the passages nearer the entrance. Easy to lose bearings here. A side branch, easy to walk past without noticing, leads to the Shattered Expanse Portal Alcove.

**Shattered Expanse Portal Alcove**  
_Connects to: Deep Tunnel D only (dead end)_  
A small, unremarkable alcove that happens to house the portal onward to the Shattered Expanse. Nothing marks it as significant from the tunnel — no light, no formation, no Guild signage. Easy to miss entirely without knowing it's there.

**Deep Tunnel E**  
_Connects to: Main Hall ↔ Winding Passage_  
A second route away from Main Hall, low-lit and looping, designed to disorient more than threaten.

**Winding Passage**  
_Connects to: Deep Tunnel E ↔ Transitional Passage_  
Doubles back near Deep Tunnel D at points without ever connecting to it, reinforcing the sense of a confusing, half-mapped lower reach of the Hollow.

**Transitional Passage**  
_Connects to: Deep Tunnel D, Winding Passage ↔ Hidden Portal Chamber_  
Short but unmistakably wrong — runework and construction material that does not belong in a natural cave system. Sparse, unsettling details accumulate here more than anywhere else: an oversized bone fragment pressed into the wall, a marking too large for any known Hollow inhabitant.

**Hidden Portal Chamber**  
_Connects to: Transitional Passage only_  
Houses the artificial portal proper — unmistakably constructed, leading onward to an The Root Cradle1. Who built it, and why, remains unanswered.

**Narrow Vent**  
_Connects to: Stalactite Passage (up) ↔ Lower Passage (down)_  
A tight vertical squeeze requiring careful climbing. Not passable in a hurry or in heavy armour.

**Lower Passage**  
_Connects to: Narrow Vent ↔ Sunken Chamber_  
Damp, low-ceilinged, and dimmer than the levels above — a different, sparser strain of bioluminescent fungus grows here.

**Sunken Chamber**  
_Connects to: Lower Passage ↔ Flooded Alcove_  
A small chamber with standing water pooling at its lowest point. Quiet enough to suggest nothing living has claimed it recently.

**Flooded Alcove**  
_Connects to: Sunken Chamber only (dead end)_  
Standing water fills most of the floor. The end of the Lower Level route — nothing beyond it, only what's left here to find.
### Portals
The Sunken Hollow has two portals:
- **The [[Solasfall]] [[Rift]]** — The natural entry point, connecting directly to [[Solasfall]]. All explorers enter the [[Hollowdeep]] here first.
- **The Shattered Expanse Portal** — A secondary portal leading onward into the wider [[Hollowdeep]] web, opening into the Shattered Expanse.
- **The Hidden Portal** — A second, artificially constructed portal concealed deeper in the Hollow. Unlike the natural Expanse connection, this portal is unmistakably made — runework and material that does not belong in a natural cave. It leads via a short transitional passage directly to another nexus, bypassing the Expanse's routing entirely. Who built it, and why anyone would want a private route around the Guild's controlled entry point, is presently unknown.

### Ecosystem
The Sunken Hollow supports a small, self-contained food web built around its bioluminescent growth. Every carnivorous creature in this chain is opportunistic — there is no clean hierarchy, only a web where size, numbers, and circumstance decide outcomes.

- **Bioluminescent moss and fungi** — the base of the chain, food for Aphids and Caterpillars.
- **Aphids and Caterpillars** — non-combat ambient wildlife, tended by the ant colony.
- **[[Ant Worker|Ant Workers]] and [[Ant Soldier|Ant Soldiers]]** — a poly-queen *Formica Rufa* colony that tends the aphid herds and raids for food, including [[Cockroach|cockroaches]] and stray prey.
- **[[Cockroach|Cockroaches]]** — scavengers, hunted by the ants and larger predators alike, surviving through avoidance rather than combat strength.
- **[[Mantis]]** — an ambush predator preying opportunistically on ants and cockroaches.
- **[[Tarantula]]** — the apex predator within this scope, preying on everything below it. If weakened or killed, the ant colony may opportunistically consume it in turn, closing the loop.

This is not a true apex ecosystem — deeper nexuses contain things that would prey on the Tarantula without hesitation. The Sunken Hollow is simply where the chain currently ends, as far as anyone has mapped.

### Tone
Creatures here are designed to feel uncanny rather than alien — recognisable as cave fauna, but subtly and consistently wrong in ways shaped by the [[Hollowdeep]]'s [[Mana|mana]] density. This is intentional: as explorers travel deeper into the web, the wrongness should compound, with the Sunken Hollow serving as the baseline players measure everything stranger against.
