### Overview
The Cultists are [[Solasfall]]'s forbidden congregations — secret sects devoted to the three Forbidden Deities: The Maw Below, The Red Thorn, and The Hollow Shade. Unlike the Sanctums, they hold no legal standing, no public shrines, and no protection from the law. Discovery means persecution, imprisonment, or death. Despite this, worship persists in hidden rooms, abandoned tunnels, and quiet gatherings across every district, drawn together by the same desperation, obsession, or curiosity that draws people to any Faith — just without anywhere sanctioned to put it.

### Why People Turn to Forbidden Worship
Cultists are rarely drawn by malice. Most arrive through grief, hunger, obsession, or a need for secrets and power the Sanctums cannot or will not offer. The Weighing, the Ministry's extortionary doctrine, and the general precarity of life in the Slum and Commoner Districts produce a steady, quiet supply of the desperate and the curious. A follower of the Maw Below may have started as someone who simply could not stop wanting more. A follower of the Red Thorn may have begun by wanting to forget, or to be remembered by someone who no longer could.

### Structure & Organisation
Cult cells are small, secretive, and compartmentalised by necessity rather than design choice — exposure is fatal, so cells rarely know of each other even within worship of the same deity. There is no citywide unifying structure for "the Cultists" as a whole; the term is a catch-all used by outsiders (the Veiled Watch, the Sanctums, ordinary citizens) for a phenomenon that is really dozens of unconnected, unaffiliated congregations.

### The Three Cults

**The Maw Below Cult** — Worship centred on hunger, obsession, and insatiable desire. Shrines are hidden underground, in abandoned ruins, or beneath the city, often marked by blood or devoured offerings. Followers tend toward hoarders, the power-hungry, and those who have lost themselves to a specific craving. Internal cult dynamics tend to be competitive and individualistic — the deity rewards taking, not sharing, and cultists reflect this even amongst each other.

**The Red Thorn Cult** — Worship centred on forbidden love, secrets, and memory manipulation. Operates through quiet infiltration of social and personal networks rather than fixed shrines — clandestine meetings, whispered secrets, carefully placed influence over relationships and memory. Followers include spies, secret lovers, and those seeking to forget or alter painful memories. The cult's structure is relational rather than hierarchical; loyalty runs through personal bonds and secrets held over one another as much as through shared devotion.

**The Hollow Shade Cult** — Worship centred on shadows, lost souls, and forgotten truths. Operates from hidden rooms, abandoned tunnels, and secret societies, favouring obscurity and unseen guidance over direct [[Actions|action]]. Followers include assassins, outcasts, and seekers of forbidden knowledge. The cult trades in secrets and paranoia, often knowing more about the city's hidden business than it should.

### Relationships
- **The Sanctums** — treat all three cults as heretical corruption of true Faith; Sanctum clergy occasionally cooperate with the Veiled Watch to root out suspected cultists among their own congregations.
- **Order of the Veiled Watch** — primary hunters of confirmed cult activity within the city.
- **Noble Faction** — a small number of noble houses maintain clandestine ties to specific cults for leverage, blackmail material, or personal obsession, never openly.
- **The Rebel Group** — no ideological alignment, occasional overlap in membership; rebels are wary of being associated with cult activity, which would delegitimise their grievances in the public eye.
- **The Thieves' Guild** — informal, transactional overlap around smuggling forbidden texts, relics, and materials.

### Public Perception
Ordinary citizens fear and avoid confirmed cult activity, though rumour and superstition about "what really happens" in the city's forgotten spaces run far ahead of any confirmed fact. Most citizens have never knowingly met a cultist and would not recognise one if they had.
