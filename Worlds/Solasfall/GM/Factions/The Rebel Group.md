### Overview
The Rebel Group is [[Solasfall]]'s loosest and least understood faction — not a single organisation but a name applied, by outsiders and insiders alike, to a scattered network of grassroots cells united by opposition to the Ministry of Balance and, more broadly, to a system that taxes the poor to fund a miracle they're told they should be grateful for. Nobody outside the [[Movement Types|movement]] — and few within it — can say with confidence whether it has one leader, several, or none at all. That uncertainty isn't an accident.

### Composition & Recruitment
Overwhelmingly grassroots. No noble houses bankroll the Rebels out of belief — the few noble ties that exist are transactional, houses quietly funding or informing a cell for leverage against a rival, not out of shared conviction. Recruitment draws almost entirely from the Slum and Commoner Districts: families broken by Weighing executions, debtors who fled the Gilded Chain, workers who watched a neighbour conscripted into a [[Hollowdeep]] expedition to cover a quota they couldn't pay. Grief and debt are the [[Movement Types|movement]]'s real recruiters, not ideology.

### Structure
Cells operate in near-total isolation from one another, deliberately mirroring (whether by design or convergent necessity) the compartmentalisation the Veiled Watch uses against them. A member of one cell may not know whether other cells exist, let alone who runs them. This produces the [[Movement Types|movement]]'s defining mystery: is there a hidden coordinating hand, or is "the Rebel Group" simply dozens of unconnected groups sharing a name and an enemy? Both rebels and the authorities who hunt them are genuinely divided on the answer.

### Internal Politics — The [[Veil]] Fracture
The [[Movement Types|movement]]'s one well-known internal divide, discussed openly even among sympathetic citizens: some rebels believe true freedom lies beyond the [[Veil]], not understanding or refusing to believe the time acceleration waiting outside. Others know exactly what the [[Veil]] holds back and fear that weakening it for any reason — even to strike at the Ministry — courts total catastrophe. This split occasionally turns cells against each other as much as against the city, and no unifying rebel doctrine has ever resolved it.

### Tactics
Sabotage of Ministry tax infrastructure, disruption of Weighing collection routes, freeing condemned debtors before Execution Day, and targeted killing of Gilded Chain enforcers where retaliation can be afforded. Counterpropaganda — pamphlets and word-of-mouth attacking the "Balance is Survival" doctrine as extortion dressed in civic virtue — is as much a [[Weapon|weapon]] as any blade.

### Relationships
- **The Ministry of Balance** — the primary target and the thing that gives the [[Movement Types|movement]] whatever coherence it has.
- **Order of the Gilded Chain** — direct, frequent, and lethal enemies.
- **Order of the Veiled Watch** — the Rebels' most dangerous hunter; cell structure exists largely because of them.
- **The Thieves' Guild** — informal, situational cooperation on smuggling, forged papers, and safehouses, never formal alliance.
- **Cultists** — no ideological alignment, occasional overlap in membership (the same desperation that produces a rebel can produce a cultist), treated with wariness by rebels who don't want the [[Movement Types|movement]]'s grievances confused with heresy.
- **Nobility** — a small number of houses maintain clandestine, transactional contact for political leverage, not belief.
- **The Explorers' Guild** — not a target by default, but Guild-escorted parties or contracted Knights sent into rebel territory are treated as hostile the moment they appear armed and searching.

### Public Perception
Sympathy is real but quiet in the poorer districts — most citizens who'd never join a cell still won't inform on one. The wealthy and the Ministry itself frame the Rebels as chaos for its own sake, criminal opportunists exploiting the Weighing's hardship rather than responding to it. Nobody — rebel, citizen, or authority — actually knows the true shape of the organisation, and that uncertainty is treated as its own kind of threat.

### Symbol & Motto
No single sanctioned symbol — cells mark territory or solidarity with whatever improvised sign suits them, a broken scale scratched into stone being the most recurring, a deliberate mockery of the Ministry's own emblem. No agreed motto exists publicly.

### The Hidden Hand (GM Only — Canon)
*Name, appearance, and current alias: #Wip*

A single figure sits at the quiet centre of an unknown number of cells — not publicly acknowledged, not confirmed to exist even by most rebels who unknowingly answer to structures he has influenced. His hatred is specific: not the [[Veil]], not the city itself, but the Ministry of Balance and the doctrine that dresses extortion as sacred necessity.

**On the [[Veil]]:** He has no interest in bringing it down. He understands exactly what that would cost and mass death was never his goal. This places him, functionally, on the preserve-the-[[Veil]] side of the [[Movement Types|movement]]'s internal fracture — though he never declares this outright. A divided [[Movement Types|movement]] is a [[Movement Types|movement]] no single Veiled Watch cell can fully map, and he has no incentive to resolve the split even where he privately holds a position on it.

**The Contradiction:** He kills Knights and Explorers sent to hunt him, framing it as necessary defence of the cause — the same "necessary sacrifice for the greater good" logic he despises in the Ministry's own justification for the Weighing. Whether he recognises this hypocrisy in himself, and whether it ever surfaces to challenge him from within his own ranks, is left open. This is a live narrative hook rather than a resolved character trait — a PC, a rival cell leader, or a former ally could plausibly be the one who eventually names it to his face.

**Reveal Timing:** Deliberately unscheduled. Player discovery of his existence, identity, or specific cells under his influence should surface organically through play rather than a planned beat — the GM introduces him when the moment in-session earns it, not on a predetermined timeline.
