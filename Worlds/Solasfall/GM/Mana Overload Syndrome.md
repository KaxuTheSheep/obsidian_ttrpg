[[Mana]] Overload Syndrome occurs when [[Mana|mana]] flow through a [[Soul|soul]] exceeds what the neurological structures generating it can withstand. The [[Soul|soul]] does not store [[Mana|mana]] — it conducts it. Under normal conditions the flow passes through and dissipates. Under extraordinary conditions the volume or excitation intensity of that flow becomes catastrophic, permanently damaging the neurological architecture the [[Soul|soul]] depends on.

## Cause
Two factors determine whether [[Mana|mana]] flow becomes lethal to the [[Soul|soul]]:

**Volume** is the primary cause. Forcing an extraordinary quantity of [[Mana|mana]] through a single [[Soul|soul]] — the kind required by civilisational scale magical undertakings — overwhelms the neurological structures regardless of excitation state. Normal casting, regardless of enhancement investment or failed control, does not approach this threshold. Backlash, Exhaustion, and Sanity [[Damage Types|damage]] are the consequences of ordinary magical failure. [[Mana]] Overload requires something far beyond that.

**Excitation intensity** acts as a multiplier. Highly excited [[Mana|mana]] exerts greater force on the neurological structures conducting it, producing the same catastrophic effect at lower volume than inert or weakly excited [[Mana|mana]] would require. This is the mechanism behind stone-related overloads — a highly excited [[Mana]] Stone failing or being forced against its excitation state releases a concentrated burst of intensely charged [[Mana|mana]]. A skilled mage has limited reaction time to withdraw before the spike passes through their [[Soul|soul]].

The two factors compound. High volume of highly excited [[Mana|mana]] is the most dangerous combination possible, as demonstrated by the [[Veil]]'s sealing ritual.

## Consequences
[[Mana]] Overload Syndrome is binary. The neurological structures either survive intact or they do not. There are no partial outcomes, no degrees of recovery, no gradual rehabilitation.

When overload occurs the consequences are permanent and total:
- All ability to conduct [[Mana|mana]] is lost. No casting of any kind is possible.
- All perception of [[Mana|mana]] is extinguished. The passive awareness through which mages experience ambient [[Mana|mana]] flow — the persistent background sense that defines a trained mage's relationship with the world — is gone entirely. Not diminished. Gone.
- Depending on the scale of the event, physical and neurological [[Damage Types|damage]] accompanies the loss. In extreme cases the [[Damage Types|damage]] extends beyond the [[Soul|soul]]'s conducting function into broader neurological systems, leaving the mage alive but severely impaired in thought, memory, or independent function.

There is no recovery. The [[Damage Types|damage]] is structural and permanent.

## Stone Handling Risk
[[Mana]] Overload among practitioners most commonly occurs through catastrophic [[Mana]] Stone failure rather than casting. A stone that is damaged, rapidly depleted, or forced against its excitation state can fail unpredictably, releasing its charge in an uncontrolled burst through the [[Soul|soul]] of whoever is conducting [[Mana|mana]] through it at the time. Higher excitation intensity means higher risk — a weakly excited stone failing produces a manageable spike, while a highly excited stone failing can produce an overload before the practitioner can withdraw.

This is a known occupational hazard in the Explorers' Guild and the [[Magic]] Institutions. Handling protocols exist specifically to minimise forced excitation conflicts and to ensure practitioners withdraw from a stone showing structural instability before failure occurs.

## The King
The most prominent known case is the current King of [[Solasfall]], who suffered catastrophic overload during the final sealing ritual of the [[Veil]]. The volume of [[Mana|mana]] conducted through a single [[Soul|soul]] during that moment was without precedent — the scale required to seal a structure encompassing the entire city in a single ritual act. The neurological [[Damage Types|damage]] extended beyond his conducting ability into broader function, leaving him alive but absent — incapable of governance or independent thought.

His condition is officially described as a severe illness. He rarely appears in public. When he does, magical means are used to present a convincing performance of health and presence. The Royal Advisors govern [[Solasfall]] entirely in his name. Few outside the innermost circle of power know the truth.
