# Ship Materials & Shuttle Combat Mechanics (GM Only)

This expands the player-facing shuttle combat overview with the corp-specific material identities. These are **defaults to build the core system from** — more material types (salvaged, mercenary, alien-influenced) can and should be added later.

## Core Loop Recap
- **Raw material** (reserve pool) converts into **Ready-to-use material** over time at a defined conversion rate.
- Incoming [[Damage Types|damage]] is offset by ready-to-use material first.
- A shuttle actually goes down when sustained, accurate fire outpaces conversion rate faster than the shipwright can compensate — not from a single big hit.
- Focused fire on one location beats passive regeneration; spread fire tests total capacity instead.

## Corp Material Defaults

**Halvorsen Genomics — organic/bio-grown hulls**
- Passive self-heal over time (Engineer-ship style regeneration), even outside active shipwright attention.
- Vulnerable to repeated/precision strikes on the same location — regeneration falters if the same spot keeps getting hit.
- Thematically weaker against Fire and Acid [[Damage Types|damage types]] (organic material read as flammable/corrodible) — use as flavour/GM discretion until formal resistances are needed.

**Kurosawa-Faulkner — synthetic/printed hulls**
- Fast, consistent raw-to-ready conversion rate — efficient, reliable, no biological limitations.
- No passive regeneration and a lower capacity ceiling than the other two — unspectacular but dependable, matching the "outlet, not individual" company philosophy.

**Ossuary Dynamics — integrated/smart-material hulls (players' default)**
- Highest ready-to-use capacity and/or reactive hardening under impact (armour that stiffens in response, tied to neural-response integration — Ossuary's core specialism).
- Costlier raw material and/or slower baseline conversion rate as the trade-off.

## Player Ships
Players default to an Ossuary-built hull but are never locked to it — full respec of weapons, materials, and geometry is intended as an ongoing progression path, especially as salvage/capture from rival corp ships becomes available through boarding [[Actions|actions]].

## Design Note
Keep exact numbers (conversion rates, capacity values, DR figures) to be defined during actual system-building — this document captures the *relationships* between the three material types (regen-but-exploitable vs reliable-but-flat vs high-capacity-but-costly) so future numeric balancing has a clear identity to hit for each.
