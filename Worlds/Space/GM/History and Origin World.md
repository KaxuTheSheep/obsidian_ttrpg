# History & the Origin World (GM Only)

## The Origin World
A founding coalition, originating from Earth, built a single origin world as the result of a space race: a Dyson swarm of detachable, modular city-segments — modules can be physically disconnected and relocated, making the "planet" a mutable, engineered structure rather than a fixed place. Most of its early population were raised from embryo aboard the swarm itself, never having set foot on Earth.

Over time, as the coalition expanded outward for resources, the arrangement calcified into today's three corporations, each running its own separate Dyson swarm system elsewhere, each trying to replicate the origin world's success on their own terms — so far, none successfully.

The origin world itself still exists, presumably still populated and still politically relevant, though it is not one of the three corp factions itself — treat it as a background power/legacy authority with its own interests, potentially useful as a neutral-ish location or as a source of "old world" legitimacy any corp might want to invoke.

## The Players' Outer Colony
Before their current lives, the player characters were Casts — manufactured by Halvorsen Genomics, deployed as slave labour on an outer colony world within one corp's Dyson swarm system (a planet, not the swarm/origin world itself). Biological Casts were used there because certain frontier/colony work was more efficient with organic bodies than androids or pure synthetics could provide.

The player characters escaped this life together, became factionless defectors, and were subsequently contracted by a rival corporation — currently Ossuary Dynamics. Full brain-casing conversion was partly practical (frontier work benefits from it) and partly deliberate erasure: removing the organic body destroys the visible product markers (the under-eye 3D QR code) that would otherwise make them identifiable/reclaimable by their original owner.

### GM Hooks
- The colony they escaped from, and its parent corp, can absolutely resurface — old owners rarely forget product loss, especially product that's since proven capable of surviving on its own.
- Other escaped Casts from the same colony/line may exist, with wildly different outcomes — some captured and returned, some thriving elsewhere, some hunting the players for their own reasons.
- The specific Cast model/line the players were built from is one of the lines "known to sometimes develop human awareness" — worth deciding, if/when it matters at the table, whether that's common knowledge, a closely guarded secret, or something Halvorsen itself doesn't fully understand.
