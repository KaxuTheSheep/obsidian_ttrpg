# Exotic Matter, Energy, and the Extraction Economy

## Production Chain
1. A corp identifies a candidate star system and establishes a hub world.
2. The hub world seeds a Dyson swarm around the system's star.
3. The swarm's collected energy is transmitted to dedicated orbital collector satellites.
4. Those satellites reverse the energy into **negative energy**, which is then split into **exotic matter** and **anti-exotic matter**.
5. Exotic matter is transported back for use — wormhole maintenance, Alcubierre Drive fitting, or stockpiling.

Exotic matter production scales directly with swarm output. A corp with a larger, more efficient, or longer-established swarm produces exotic matter faster — this is the real axis of competitive advantage between the three corps, not secret technology. All three know *how* to make exotic matter; none of them can make as much of it as they'd like.

## Uses of Exotic Matter (keep this narrow)
- **Wormholes**: consumed continuously while a wormhole is active (per time, not per trip). This is why wormholes run on schedules rather than staying open — leaving one active is expensive. A corp can weaponise scheduling/access as a political tool without ever declaring war.
- **Alcubierre Drive**: consumed proportional to distance travelled. Not per-trip fixed cost, not unlimited range — a long haul costs more exotic matter, full stop. This keeps Alcubierre travel rare and precious even for the corps that can afford it at all.

Exotic matter is *not* a fuel source, not used in weapons, and not used in conventional ship engines. Keep its narrative role singular: it's what makes wormholes and warp travel possible, nothing else. This keeps corp competition legible — everyone's fighting over the same specific thing for the same specific reasons.

## Antimatter
All three corps can produce antimatter in bulk — this is not a scarcity point in the same way exotic matter is. Antimatter and fusion torch drives are the standard propulsion methods for ordinary ships (including the players'), used for near-light interstellar hauls and cheaper in-system fusion-only travel respectively.

## Adventure Hooks
- Sabotaging a rival's collector satellites (not the hub planet itself) is a precise, plausible, high-value sabotage target.
- A missed or deliberately denied wormhole activation window can strand a crew, delay a mission, or be used as corporate leverage.
- A rare, high-speed Alcubierre-fitted ship appearing somewhere it shouldn't be is an immediate signal that something urgent or important is happening.
