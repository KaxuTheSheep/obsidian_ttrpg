# 36 Ophiuchi Ab — Planetary Profile

**Scientific designation:** 36 Ophiuchi Ab (orbits the outer component, 36 Oph C, of the real triple K-dwarf system 36 Ophiuchi)

## Star & Orbit
- **Star type:** K-dwarf (orange, dimmer/cooler than Sol), pale yellow-orange in the sky.
- **Orbit:** Inner edge of the habitable zone — net noticeably hotter than Earth despite the dimmer star.
- **Companion stars:** 36 Oph A and B (a close binary pair) are visible as an extremely bright "wandering star" pair, moving against the sky over the local year rather than fixed like a moon.
  - **True darkness is rare.** Most nights have some binary illumination (dim, cool, paler-toned light).
  - Native fauna stealth/camouflage has adapted to *expect* ambient binary light on most nights — true darkness is the unusual event, not the norm.
  - Consequence: players' Earth-honed instinct that "night = hidden" is actively wrong here. On binary-lit nights, native life reads motion and shape easily; unadapted visitors stand out.
- **Rotation:** Fast — a day is roughly **17 hours**. Combined with the near-constant binary illumination, native life rarely commits to strict diurnal/nocturnal scheduling; activity windows tend to be opportunistic rather than fixed to a light/dark cycle.

## Physical Characteristics
- **Radius:** 0.9x Earth.
- **Gravity:** ~0.9g (radius-driven; core density is Earth-normal, not deficient).
- **Core:** Normal/Earth-like composition, proportionally smaller due to smaller planet radius.
- **Magnetosphere:** Weaker than Earth's, and weaker still than a typical smaller-core world would suggest — offset by the dimmer star, so net radiation exposure remains background flavour rather than a plot-critical hazard. The reduced field is one reason native fauna lean away from magnetoreception as a navigational sense (see Fauna Overview) in favour of acoustic mapping and celestial (binary-light) cues.
- **Crust:** Granite and limestone dominant. Karst geology — caves, sinkholes, underground rivers, natural acoustic chambers.
- **Tectonic/geological activity:** More active than Earth, though not extreme — expressed mainly as elevated hydrogen sulfide venting through the karst system (feeding the sulfur-chemosynthesis food web) plus scattered minor geothermal pockets deep in the cave network. Not a source of large-scale volcanism or a separate energy economy — the extra activity intensifies the existing sulfur cycle rather than introducing a new one.

## Seasons & Temperature
- **No meaningful temperature-driven seasons.** Axial tilt is minimal; surface temperature stays broadly stable year-round.
- **Seasonality instead expressed as humidity cycles** — distinct wetter and drier seasons. Even the "dry" season stays well short of arid or temperate-world dryness; it's a relative easing of an otherwise very humid baseline, not a true dry season.
- **Average surface temperature:** roughly **30–34°C**, with meaningful variance by vertical zone — canopy-top running hottest (35–40°C, direct light exposure), floor and cave systems cooler and more thermally stable (~24–28°C), consistent with their insulation from surface heat.
- **No polar ice caps.** The planet's stable, high heat and lack of temperature-driven seasons don't support permanent ice anywhere on the surface.

## Hydrology
- **Ocean coverage: ~30%**, land-dominant relative to Earth.
- **Salinity: notably higher than Earth's oceans.** With comparatively little ocean volume to dilute into, and an otherwise intact water cycle (rivers carrying dissolved minerals and salts from the karst-heavy landmass out to sea), dissolved salt content concentrates more heavily in the smaller ocean basin.
- **Most of the planet's water budget is not in the ocean at all** — it's held in canopy/rainforest biomass and as atmospheric humidity (consistent with the 5% atmospheric water vapour figure below), rather than in open water. This is a land-and-air-dominated water cycle, not an ocean-dominated one.

## Atmosphere
**Surface pressure:** ~1.9 atm (crushing/pressure-hazardous unshielded — sealed, pressure-rated suits mandatory).

| Component | % by Volume | Partial Pressure (approx) | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Argon | 54% | ~1.03 atm | Primary inert bulk gas — density driver |
| Nitrogen | 20% | ~0.38 atm | Minor but biologically necessary (nitrogen fixation) |
| Oxygen | 17% | ~0.32 atm | Elevated vs Earth's 0.21 atm — supports high bioproductivity |
| Water vapour | 5% | ~0.095 atm | High ambient humidity baseline |
| Trace gases | ~4% | ~0.076 atm | See below |

**Trace gases (~4% total):**
- **Carbon dioxide** — ~20% above Earth's current level (still a small overall fraction)
- **Ammonia (NH₃)** — trace; pungent, filtered by suits
- **Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S)** — trace at the atmospheric level, though locally much higher near karst vents; geologically vented from the karst/limestone system, filtered, source of the "wet cave" smell
- **Methane** — small trace; plausible byproduct of high floor-layer decomposition and biological activity
- **Neon** — minor residual trace, realism padding, no biological/narrative relevance

**Practical effect:** Suits must be pressure-rated (crush hazard) and filter ammonia, H₂S, excess CO2, and methane. The underlying Ar/N2/O2 balance is fundamentally breathable once processed — air is "breathable after filtering," not requiring full atmosphere replacement.

**Density note:** Argon (atomic mass ~40) is denser than nitrogen (~28), so the atmosphere is meaningfully heavier per breath than Earth's even before accounting for pressure. This supports:
- Cheaper, more common flight-analogue locomotion (lift-assisted).
- Faster sound propagation — echolocation and resonance-based senses are meaningfully enhanced over Earth-baseline (reinforced further by karst cave acoustics). This is a major driver of native fauna sensory biology (see Fauna Overview).

## Nitrogen Scarcity — Biological Bottleneck
With nitrogen at only ~20% of the atmosphere (vs Earth's ~78%), nitrogen-fixing organisms have far less raw material per unit of air processed. Amino acid and protein synthesis is comparatively expensive across the whole biosphere. This is a deliberate, load-bearing constraint:
- Explains unusually aggressive/efficient predation.
- Explains carnivorous flora (see Flora doc) and near-universal opportunistic carnivory in fauna (see Fauna Overview).
- Explains a general tendency toward smaller-but-tougher organisms rather than large-and-wasteful ones — reinforced further by predation pressure, since the ecosystem's dense carnivory makes very large, slow-breeding animals an easy target rather than a safe niche. True megafauna does not exist here.
- Normalises scavenging/cannibalism-adjacent behaviour across trophic levels.

## Vertical Zone Structure
Four distinct zones, each with its own light, temperature/humidity, and sensory logic. Temperature/humidity offset roughly 50% between zones.

1. **Canopy-top** — Bright, direct light, hottest and most variable. Photosynthesis-driven food web.
2. **Mid-canopy** — Dim, dappled light. Sight/motion-based predation dominant.
3. **Floor/Understory** — Dark, humid, still. Echolocation-dominant. Detritus + sulfur-chemosynthesis driven food web.
4. **Karst Caves** — Cooler, stable, insulated from surface heat despite the hot climate. Own distinct fauna, including a minor geothermal-adjacent niche alongside the dominant sulfur-chemosynthesis economy. Mineral/chemolithotrophic food web.

### Zone Interconnection
Floor and cave food webs are not fully isolated — both draw on the same geological sulfur/mineral venting from the karst system, just expressed differently depending on light availability:
- **Caves:** full darkness-adapted mineral/chemolithotrophic life, minimal organic input, plus a small localised geothermal-vent niche where geological activity is concentrated.
- **Floor:** sulfur-oxidising life supplemented by organic detritus falling from the canopy above.

## Player-Facing Hazards
- **Atmospheric pressure:** Crushing without a sealed, pressure-rated suit.
- **Trace toxins:** Ammonia and H₂S require active filtration, more acutely near karst vents.
- **Augmented crew:** Lung structures converted into canister-style air reserves (only the brain requires sustained protection).
- **Synthetics:** Do not require air at all, but still need pressure-rated housings against the crush hazard.
