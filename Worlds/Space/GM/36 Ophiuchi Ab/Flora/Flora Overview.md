# 36 Ophiuchi Ab — Flora

Three loosely connected food webs rather than one unified biosphere: sunlight-driven (canopy), sulfur/detritus-driven (floor), and mineral-driven (caves). Floor and cave systems connect via shared geological sulfur venting from the karst system.

## Universal Traits
- **Pigment:** Deep blackish-purple across all light-driven (photosynthetic) flora — tuned to the K-dwarf's redder-weighted spectrum, broad absorption, minimal reflection (only violet/blue escapes). This is the visual identity of the surface biosphere.
- **Leaf/capture-structure shape** converges toward broad, thin, light-catching forms regardless of alienness — a physics constraint, not an Earth-specific quirk. Alienness expresses through texture, arrangement, and behaviour rather than fighting this baseline shape.
- **Nitrogen scarcity** (see Planetary Profile) drives widespread carnivory/predation in flora across multiple zones — a direct, load-bearing adaptation, not incidental flavour.

---

## Canopy-Top — "Sail-Growth"
**Mechanism:** Standard photosynthesis (primary energy driver of the whole surface food web).

- **Structure:** Fewer, larger sail-like membranes rather than many small leaves — broad blackish-purple panels, heliotropic (actively sun-tracking, light is precious enough here to chase rather than passively receive).
- **Surface:** Thick, waxy, faintly glossy — manages water loss under direct heat despite high ambient humidity.
- **Translucency:** Vein-channels through the sails show light passing through — purple-black body with threads of light tracing through, stained-glass effect.
- **Growth habit:** Emergent, treelike, but "trunks" are braided/fused clusters of woody vines climbing toward granite highlands and canopy gaps, competing hard for limited direct-light real estate.
- **Predation:** Minor — sticky-sail variants or snap-fold sail segments occasionally supplement photosynthesis by capturing insect/small-fauna moving through canopy gaps. "Mostly a sail, sometimes a trap."

## Mid-Canopy — "Lantern-Growth"
**Mechanism:** Photosynthesis, supplemented by minor bioluminescence-recycling (reflected/recycled light within dense leaf clusters — a genuine "every little helps" adaptation in a light-starved zone, not a primary energy source).

- **Structure:** Smaller, more numerous, cupped/bowl-shaped leaves — built to catch scattered dappled light from multiple angles rather than track one direct source.
- **Bioluminescence:** Soft internal glow (bluish/violet, matching the pigment's reflected colour) used as a light-attractant in a zone too dim for purely visual advertising otherwise.
- **Texture:** Thinner, more membranous than canopy-top — less water-loss pressure this deep, less light to justify thick waxy investment.
- **Predation:** This is the richest carnivorous zone. The bioluminescent lure mechanism is directly repurposed from pollinator-attraction to prey-attraction — lantern-growth plants lure small fauna with light, then trap them. Elegant reuse of an existing trait rather than a bolt-on adaptation.

## Floor/Understory — "Fungal-Frond"
**Mechanism:** Not photosynthetic — sulfur-oxidising chemosynthesis (metabolising H₂S vented from the karst system) supplemented by organic detritus falling from the canopy above.

- **Structure:** Branching, fan-like, filamentous — fungal or coral-like rather than leafy, optimised for nutrient absorption rather than light capture.
- **Colour:** Breaks from the purple-black motif — pale, bone-white, or faintly bioluminescent-blue, correctly signalling this is a different system from the canopy above.
- **Movement:** Slow-pulsing, filter-feeding motion (anemone/coral-like) — floor life in constant still, humid air can afford animal-like behaviour without true mobility.
- **Predation:** Sound-based trapping rather than visual — fronds mimic vibration/echo signatures to lure prey with a false "echo" of something worth investigating, fitting the zone's echolocation-dominant sensory logic. A genuinely alien hook distinct from light-based lures above.

## Karst Caves — "Mineral-Bloom"
**Mechanism:** Chemolithotrophy — iron- or sulfur-oxidising metabolism drawing directly on limestone/granite mineral content and groundwater chemistry (real-world analogue: chemolithotrophic cave bacteria, scaled up).

- **Structure:** Crystalline-adjacent, mineral-crusted growths — can be mistaken for rock formations at a glance until disturbed.
- **Colour:** Follows floor flora in breaking from purple-black — pale, mineral-toned, occasionally faint bioluminescent-blue.
- **Predation:** Largely absent. Not meaningfully competing for nitrogen the way surface flora are; slower growth, mineral/sulfur-focused metabolism reduces the evolutionary pressure toward carnivory. Not every zone needs every trait.

## Design Notes for Fauna Work
- Herbivore-analogues browsing canopy/mid-canopy flora are taking a real risk given widespread carnivorous flora — prey-animal behaviour should reflect caution and evolved counter-detection, not passive grazing.
- True darkness is rare (see Planetary Profile) — floor/cave flora lure mechanisms and fauna stealth are both tuned to *expect* ambient binary starlight on most nights, not absolute dark.
