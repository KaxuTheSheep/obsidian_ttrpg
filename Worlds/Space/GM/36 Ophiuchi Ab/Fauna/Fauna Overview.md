# 36 Ophiuchi Ab — Fauna

All fauna on 36 Ophiuchi Ab trace back to the same single abiogenesis event as the planet's flora (see [[Flora Overview]]). This means biochemistry, sensory fundamentals, and skin structure are **universal** — shared by every animal and plant on the planet — while body structure, behaviour, and ecology are free to diverge into distinct **body plans**. Two body plans currently cover the vast majority of known fauna, with more room to add others later.

---

## Universal Fauna Traits

These traits are true of *all* fauna regardless of body plan, and where noted, shared with flora as a consequence of common ancestry.

### Skin
Every fauna and flora species carries an outer, whitish-translucent skin/cuticle layer sitting above the actual pigment-bearing cell layer beneath it. This is an ancestral-aquatic trait; native life generally lacks the kind of dense, moisture-locking skin common on drier-adapted Earth animals, because the planet's baseline humidity has rarely made that necessary.

- **Moisture-reactive:** In wet conditions skin Veil stays soft and thin, closer to amphibian skin. In drier conditions — such as the planet's (still comparatively mild) dry season, or a human-humidity environment like a station or ship — it **hardens into a shell-like, moisture-sealing layer within a few minutes**.
- **Imperfect sealing:** The hardened form is not a true adaptation to genuine dryness — it noticeably underperforms at retaining moisture compared to Earth animals adapted to arid or temperate climates. A hardened creature is protected, not solved; it will still dehydrate over time in a dry environment and needs water replenished periodically. This is most acutely a problem for native fauna removed from their home biome entirely — e.g. aboard a ship or station — where the low-humidity environment forces them to actively seek out water they'd rarely have needed to think about at home.
- **Pigment beneath the Skin:** In flora, the underlying pigment layer is permanently active, performing photosynthesis (see [[Flora Overview]] for the deep purple-black spectral tuning). In fauna, the same pigment layer is **actuable** — creatures can switch it on and off, and can activate it **partially or in patterns**, not just as a whole-body toggle. Activation is cheap, fast, and does not interfere with echolocation. Known uses include:
  - **Camouflage/mimicry** against flora, matching the same purple-black tuning.
  - **Light/heat management** — the pigment absorbs broadly and reflects minimally, so activating it plausibly affects heat load, not just colour.
  - **Display/signalling** between individuals.
  
  Pigment mimicry is a real but secondary layer of defence — most fauna use echolocation and sight together, and echolocation in particular reads shape, presence, and movement regardless of surface colour. Pigment camouflage helps most against sight-reliant observers, or as a supplement alongside stillness.

### Sensory Suite
Sight and echolocation are **co-equal senses**, not primary/backup — they resolve different information and are used together rather than one substituting for the other.

- **Sight:** 6 eyes, head-fixed. Individual eyes commonly specialise by role (depth perception, peripheral/lookout, motion detection, etc.) rather than all six being identical or forward-facing.
- **Echolocation & breathing:** The mouth is used exclusively for feeding — vocalisation and breathing are handled entirely separately. Fauna have **4 lungs, each with a single external hole**, positioned at ancestral gill sites rather than clustered at the head: by default, one pair sits on the underside, angled forward and down, and one pair sits near the tail, angled forward and up. This default reflects a resting/typical posture — actual coverage shifts as a creature's stance changes, meaning posture itself affects echolocation coverage. The four-point spread gives genuine directional, triangulated sensing (front-to-back, up-and-down), not a single head-mounted cone.
- **Hearing/reception:** Not localised ears. Instead, **frill-like branching skin structures** — closer to pulled, feathered, or branching skin than hair — are distributed across the body at intervals, functioning as a body-wide acoustic reception array rather than two fixed points. Universal to all body plans, though reliance on this sense versus direct substrate-vibration sensing varies by lineage (see Body Plan B).
- **Magnetoreception:** Present in only a handful of lineages and vestigial where it exists — the planet's weak magnetosphere makes it an unreliable primary sense. Fauna instead navigate using acoustic mapping and the near-constant binary starlight as a positional reference.

### Biochemistry
- **Nitrogen-conservative structural materials:** given atmospheric nitrogen scarcity (see Planetary Profile), fauna avoid nitrogen-expensive structural compounds (e.g. chitin). Structural reinforcement instead comes from mineral deposition (calcium/silica) into organic tissue, echoing the planet's karst geology.
- **Aggressive nitrogen recycling:** waste nitrogen is reclaimed rather than excreted where possible. Scavenging and predation of nitrogen-rich tissue — including conspecifics — is ecologically normalised, not a rare or taboo behaviour.
- **Near-universal opportunistic/facultative carnivory:** strict herbivory is rarely energetically viable under this nitrogen economy. Most fauna are *facultative* carnivores or omnivores regardless of their nominal feeding strategy — a filter-feeder will take meat when available, a hunter will scavenge or graze when it can't get meat. Niche specialisation is weak and situational rather than fixed; it's generally more useful to ask what a species eats *when it can't get meat* than what it "primarily" eats.
- **Blood:** a sulfur-compound-based gas carrier (a polysulfide/thiol-family binding chemistry, shifting sulfur oxidation states to bind and release oxygen) rather than a metal-binding pigment like Earth's iron or the more common alien standby of copper. This ties fauna biochemistry directly into the planet's abundant, biologically active sulfur cycle (H₂S venting, chemosynthesis) rather than borrowing an Earth-style solution. Blood is a distinctive **milky, pale blue** — cloudy rather than clear, consistent with a carrier dissolved in plasma.

### Body Economy & Life History
- **Short lifespans, high reproduction, large broods** is the dominant reproductive strategy across the ecosystem — a consequence of universal predation pressure and general prey turnover. Other strategies (smaller broods, higher parental investment) do exist in specific lineages, but true low-reproduction (K-selected) strategies are rare planet-wide, not just for any one body plan.
- **Size range:** insect-scale up to a little taller than a human. **No megafauna** — heavier predation pressure makes large slow-breeding animals an easy target rather than a safe niche, and the flora itself (see [[Flora Overview]]) doesn't support a food web capable of sustaining megafauna, despite the planet's slightly lower gravity technically allowing for larger builds.

### Vertical Mobility
Given the planet's vertically-stratified biome (canopy-top → mid-canopy → floor → caves) and dense, lift-cheap atmosphere, some capability for vertical movement is a near-universal pressure across fauna — but the **mechanism is lineage-specific, not prescribed**. Gliding membranes, pneumatic/lift tissue, advanced climbing adaptations, or other solutions are all valid expressions of the same underlying pressure.

## Body Plan A — "Strider"

The generalist, motile chassis. Dominant across canopy, mid-canopy, and floor zones. Built for the ecosystem's opportunistic, non-niche-locked predation — the same body plan can flex between hunting, climbing, scavenging, and fleeing without a structural redesign.

### Structure
- **Symmetry:** Bilateral.
- **Segmentation:** Three body segments — head, thorax, abdomen.
- **Limbs:** **8 ligament-based limbs, always present** in some form — vestigial or heavily modified in some lineages, but never entirely absent. Limb function is modular: the same 8-limb, 4-segment, fully-rotational-joint architecture (see below) can be shaped by different lineages into legs, arms, climbing hooks, wings, or other appendages without changing the underlying joint mechanics.
  - **Distribution:** 6 limbs from the thorax (the primary cluster — locomotion, manipulation, and most of a lineage's functional variance lives here), 2 from the abdomen (rearmost, tail-adjacent).

### Skeleton
- **Type:** Endoskeleton, cartilage-based with selective mineral hardening — flexible cartilage throughout, with calcium/silica deposition concentrated at high-stress points (spine core, joints, limb attachment sites) rather than uniform rigid bone. Lighter than true bone; consistent with the skin's aquatic-ancestral origin and the universal vertical-mobility pressure (a lighter frame favours both climbing and gliding).
- **Spine:** One continuous, flexible spine with a mineralised core, running through all three body segments — the body bends as one continuous curve, with no wasp-waist-style segment hinging.
- **Limb joints:** Each limb is segmented **4 times**, and **every joint is fully rotational** (multi-axis, not a hinge). Segments themselves are rigid (bone-hardened cartilage), so limbs read visually as extremely well-articulated insect legs — long rigid segments connected by fully rotational joints — rather than anything octopus-like or boneless. This joint architecture is the mechanical reason modular limb function is plausible across lineages: reshaping a limb's role is a matter of segment proportion and end-structure, not reinventing the joint type.

### Muscle & Hydraulics
- **Joint drive:** Each joint is wrapped in a **radial muscle array** — a ring of short muscle bundles arranged around the joint's circumference, rather than long paired antagonist muscles running the segment length. This allows independent multi-directional rotation at each joint, which a simple antagonist pair (as in vertebrate hinge joints) couldn't drive.
- **Two separate fluid systems:**
  1. **Blood** — the sulfur-based circulatory system described above, handling gas exchange (feeding the 4 lungs), nutrient transport, and nitrogen recycling. Driven by **one primary heart**, centrally located in the thorax.
  2. **Hydraulics** — a fully separate fluid system dedicated to actuating the radial muscle arrays at each joint, functioning as a true hydraulic actuator layer on top of the muscle rather than muscle force alone. Driven by **two hydraulic hearts**, split along the same 6+2 limb grouping as the skeleton: one heart pressurising the 6-limb thoracic cluster, one pressurising the 2-limb abdominal pair. This split means localised injury to one hydraulic heart can selectively impair one limb group without affecting the other.

### Senses
Standard universal sensory suite applies without lineage-specific override at the body-plan level — 6 head-fixed eyes, 4-lung echolocation array at gill-ancestral sites, body-wide frill hearing structures.
