# Xenos (GM Only)

## The Planet
Xenos is a rogue planet, originally formed without a star, captured into orbit around Procyon within the last few billion years. By the time humanity finds it, it has been bathed in real sunlight for a couple of billion years — plenty of time for its life, which originated in total darkness under a subsurface ocean, to have surfaced, diversified, and evolved into forms that bear only distant, structural resemblance to its ancestry. The planet's earliest, rogue-phase history (relevant to the speculative biology reference material) is deep backstory only — fossil record and scientific curiosity, not something actively unfolding during play.

Xenos hosts the first confirmed multicellular life ever recorded outside Earth. All three corporations want a stake in it — for prestige, for biological material, for strategic footing — and none will back down. This is the one place in the setting where corp rivalry turns genuinely hot, in a contained, localised way, without threatening the wider cold-war balance elsewhere.

## Design Philosophy for Xenos Life
Two independent abiogenesis events occurred on Xenos, in the deep past, in total isolation from one another. Use these as **radix-level building blocks** — inspiration and internal logic, not hard templates. Creatures should feel unmistakably alien: not Earth-life with a reskin. See `/gm/reference/` for the full speculative biology source material (Ferrovita and Sulphovita).

**Radix 1 — mineral/electrochemical lineage (inspired by Ferrovita):**
- Body structure built from mineral lattices rather than organic polymers; can be physically continuous with rock/geology.
- Potentially extreme longevity — "death" requires actual structural destruction, not biological failure; dormancy rather than dying is the default response to hardship.
- Naturally slow — growth and reproduction over long timescales (though nothing stops evolved surface descendants from being fast; that's a big divergence point from their ancestry worth using deliberately).
- Traits to lean on for design: armoured, geological camouflage, things that look like inert terrain until they aren't, near-unkillable without deliberate, targeted destruction.

**Radix 2 — peptide/fluid lineage (inspired by Sulphovita):**
- Soft-bodied, membrane-boundaried, fast-replicating, adaptable.
- Fragile once breached — rapid death, no persistence after death (no corpses/remains to speak of, or very short-lived ones).
- Predation and rapid adaptation are established evolutionary directions already present in the source material — good basis for genuinely dangerous, fast, opportunistic Xenos predators.
- Traits to lean on for design: fast, reactive, dissolves/decomposes quickly after death, aggressive resource competition, evolves quickly (population-level adaptation could matter within a single campaign's timeframe if you want that).

**Hybrid/convergent forms** are explicitly encouraged — mineral "shell" or skeletal structure (radix 1 derived) housing or supporting fast, soft, reactive tissue (radix 2 derived). Think: something that looks like a rock formation until the soft, fast thing living inside/on top of it reacts to a threat. Also consider plant-like fixtures, structures that are technically part-mineral, part-organic without a clean line between the two.

## Ecological Theme
Consider layering one broad environmental theme across Xenos zones: **some regions are ancient and stable-hostile** (radix-1-dominant, slow, geological, deceptively inert, dangerous through scale/permanence rather than aggression), **others are volatile and rapidly evolving/dangerous** (radix-2-dominant, fast, reactive, genuinely adaptive within short timescales, dangerous through speed/unpredictability). This isn't a hard rule — the biology docs are described as building blocks, not requirements — but it's a strong, ready-made way to make different regions of the planet feel distinct to explore.

## Practical Note
Xenos monster design is intentionally scoped as its own future session — this file exists to keep the underlying logic consistent once that work starts, not to lock specific creatures yet.
