Death represents the ultimate consequence of failing to survive [[Injuries|injury]], exhaustion, or catastrophic events. In [[Solasfall]], characters do not immediately die when [[Blood]] reaches zero, but instead enter a critical state from which [[Skills|survival]] is still possible.
## Dying
When a character's [[Blood]] reaches zero, they fall unconscious and enter the dying state.
- While dying, the character gains 1 level of Exhaustion per [[Rounds|Round]].
- If Exhaustion reaches 10, the character dies permanently.
- Any [[Damage Types|damage]] that reduces [[Blood]] to zero or below converts the excess into additional Exhaustion levels, and [[Blood]] resets to zero. This applies both on the hit that causes the initial knockout and any continued [[Damage Types|damage]] taken while already dying.

> **Example:** A character with 2 [[Sturdiness]] and 5 [[Blood]] takes 10 [[Damage Types|damage]]. 2 is absorbed by [[Sturdiness]], the remaining 8 reduces [[Blood]] to 0 with 3 excess — [[Blood]] resets to 0 and the character immediately gains 3 Exhaustion levels in addition to falling unconscious.

## Stabilising the Dying
A dying character can be stabilised to prevent further Exhaustion accumulation.
- A successful [[Skills|Medicine]] Test (DN 12) by an ally stabilises the character.
- Certain spells can stabilise a character without a Test.
- Stabilised characters remain unconscious but no longer gain Exhaustion each [[Rounds|Round]].
- Restoring [[Blood]], reducing Exhaustion, and returning the character to fighting [[Conditions|condition]] requires proper medical care, [[Rest & Recovery|rest]], or [[magic]].

## Waking an Unconscious Character
An ally may attempt to wake a stabilised or unconscious character by spending 1 Full [[Actions|Action]]. This does not restore [[Blood]] or reduce Exhaustion — it simply brings the character to consciousness if they are stable enough to be roused.

## Resurrection
Permanent death can only be reversed through rare, difficult, and costly means.
- A character may only be resurrected once across their entire life.
- Resurrection typically requires powerful [[magic]], advanced rituals, or specialised services.
- This limitation maintains narrative weight and ensures death carries genuine consequence.
