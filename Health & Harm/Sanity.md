Sanity represents your character's mental steadiness — the ability to remain focused, grounded, and emotionally composed under stress. Where [[Blood]] measures the body's life force, Sanity measures the mind's resilience. As Sanity is depleted, the mind begins to fray, and stress [[reactions]], panic responses, or deeper psychological disturbances may surface.

## Calculation
**Sanity = Resolve + Class Bonus + Level Bonus + Other Modifiers**

- **Resolve:** Core mental strength and determination.
- **Class Bonus:** Some paths cultivate discipline, reflection, or mental training.
- **Level Bonus:** Each level up grants +1 to your maximum Sanity, reflecting the hardened mind of a veteran.
- **Other Modifiers:** Can arise from training, [[blessings]], [[essences]], or rare mental conditioning.

## Function
Sanity is reduced by fear, trauma, psychological strain, magical backlash, and certain [[Hollowdeep]] encounters. Losing Sanity does not represent physical [[Injuries|injury]] — it represents stress load building against the mind. When Sanity reaches critically low levels, the character is at risk of developing [[Afflictions]], lasting mental scars that shape outlook, behaviour, or emotional response.

## Sanity Thresholds

|Current Sanity|State|Mechanical Effect|
|---|---|---|
|≥75%|Steady|No mechanical penalties.|
|50–74%|Uneasy|Disadvantage on [[Skills\|Intuition]] Tests.|
|25–49%|Strained|Disadvantage on [[Skills\|Negotiation]] Tests.|
|10–24%|Fracturing|Once per encounter, when choosing to hold position in combat, make a Resolve Test.|
|1–9%|Breaking Point|Before each [[Rest & Recovery\|Rest]], make a Resolve Test vs DN set by the GM. On a failed Test, gain an [[Afflictions\|Affliction]].|
|0|Break|Gain 1 [[Afflictions\|Affliction]], then restore Sanity to 1.|
