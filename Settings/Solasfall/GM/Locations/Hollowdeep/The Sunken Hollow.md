### Overview
The Sunken Hollow is a natural cave system serving as one of the entry nexuses accessible from [[The Shattered Expanse]]. Unlike the Expanse's mirrored, disorienting geometry, the Hollow is a straightforward — if alien — stretch of natural rock: stone passages, underground water, and chambers lit by faintly bioluminescent moss and fungi growing along the walls. It is intended as a more accessible first proper destination for explorers, teaching the fundamentals of Hollowdeep survival without the perceptual hostility of deeper or stranger nexuses.

The strangeness here is restrained. Things mostly look like things should. They just aren't quite right.

### Structure
- **Entry Chamber** — A large, well-lit chamber where the portal from the Shattered Expanse opens. Bioluminescent growth is thickest here. Old Explorers' Guild markers and the remains of a previous expedition's camp can be found, offering basic orientation and narrative breadcrumbs.
- **Branching Passages** — Two or three tunnels lead from the entry chamber toward distinct medium chambers, each with its own character: one carries a shallow underground stream forming a ford, one is drier with heavy stalactite formations, one is lower-ceilinged and tight.
- **The Main Hall** — A large chamber serving as the nexus's primary setpiece. High-ceilinged with multiple entry points and a significant ledge drop, it offers strong bioluminescent lighting and room for genuine tactical movement.
- **Evidence of Something Older** — Scattered through the Hollow are bones, hollowed spaces, and markings too large for any current inhabitant. What made them is not answered here.

### Portals
The Sunken Hollow has two portals:
1. **The Shattered Expanse Portal** — The natural entry point, connecting back to [[The Shattered Expanse]].
2. **The Hidden Portal** — A second, artificially constructed portal concealed deeper in the Hollow. Unlike the natural Expanse connection, this portal is unmistakably made — runework and material that does not belong in a natural cave. It leads via a short transitional passage directly to another nexus, bypassing the Expanse's routing entirely. Who built it, and why anyone would want a private route around the Guild's controlled entry point, is presently unknown.

### Ecosystem
The Sunken Hollow supports a small, self-contained food web built around its bioluminescent growth. Every carnivorous creature in this chain is opportunistic — there is no clean hierarchy, only a web where size, numbers, and circumstance decide outcomes.

- **Bioluminescent moss and fungi** — the base of the chain, food for [[Aphids and Caterpillars]].
- **Aphids and Caterpillars** — non-combat ambient wildlife, tended by the ant colony.
- **[[Ant Worker|Ant Workers]] and [[Ant Soldier|Ant Soldiers]]** — a poly-queen *Formica Rufa* colony that tends the aphid herds and raids for food, including [[Cockroach|cockroaches]] and stray prey.
- **[[Cockroach|Cockroaches]]** — scavengers, hunted by the ants and larger predators alike, surviving through avoidance rather than combat strength.
- **[[Mantis]]** — an ambush predator preying opportunistically on ants and cockroaches.
- **[[Tarantula]]** — the apex predator within this scope, preying on everything below it. If weakened or killed, the ant colony may opportunistically consume it in turn, closing the loop.

This is not a true apex ecosystem — deeper nexuses contain things that would prey on the Tarantula without hesitation. The Sunken Hollow is simply where the chain currently ends, as far as anyone has mapped.

### Tone
Creatures here are designed to feel uncanny rather than alien — recognisable as cave fauna, but subtly and consistently wrong in ways shaped by the Hollowdeep's mana density. This is intentional: as explorers travel deeper into the web, the wrongness should compound, with the Sunken Hollow serving as the baseline players measure everything stranger against.
