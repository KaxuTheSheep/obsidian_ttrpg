*Specialists in structured mana encoding — the construction, application, and theory of magic circles and the tools built from them.*

## Overview
The Runeweavers Institution specialises in Weave casting and the creation of magic tools. Where most institutions focus on what mana can do, the Runeweavers focus on how to encode those effects into permanent or semi-permanent structures — circles, sigils, and geometric frameworks that execute a defined magical effect when activated. Their work underpins much of Solasfall's magical infrastructure and supplies the city with tools that allow non-mages to access magical effects entirely.

## Discipline
Weave casting constrains mana through physical structure rather than real-time control. A completed circle encodes instructions that mana follows upon activation — the caster defines the behaviour in advance and the structure executes it consistently. This trades flexibility for reliability, making structured magic more predictable than Flux casting at the cost of adaptability.

All institutions teach Weave casting to some degree. The Runeweavers specialise in it entirely, developing the deepest theoretical understanding of circle construction, geometric encoding, and the relationship between structural precision and magical output. Their mages are not necessarily the most powerful casters in the city — they are the most precise, and precision is what their work demands.

### Magic Tools
The institution's most significant practical output is magic tools — objects containing a prepared two-circle system that produces a defined magical effect on activation via a mechanical trigger.

The first circle functions as a gathering instruction, directing ambient mana toward the object and establishing a current within it. The second circle contains the actual spell structure, which executes when sufficient mana flow is established through the first. The trigger — a button, lever, pressure plate, or similar mechanism — initiates the sequence.

Magic tools run on ambient mana rather than the user's soul. No conducting ability is required to operate one. As long as both circles remain intact and the surrounding environment contains excited mana, the tool functions indefinitely. Destroying either circle breaks the system entirely. In environments where ambient mana has neutralised toward inert — extremely rare under normal conditions — the tool cannot draw sufficient current to activate. Higher mana density accelerates the first phase but does not alter the output of the second.

The economic and social implications of this are significant. Magic tools place magical effects in the hands of anyone who can afford them, independent of magical training. The Runeweavers control the production of the most complex and valuable tools, giving them considerable commercial influence alongside their academic role.

### Circle Recovery
A secondary but valued function of the institution is the analysis and reproduction of magic circles recovered from the Hollowdeep. Nexus environments contain circle structures of unknown origin — some functional, some inert, some partially degraded. Runeweavers accompanying expeditions document and where possible extract these structures, returning them for analysis. Successful reproduction of a recovered circle occasionally yields spell frameworks that Solasfall's own research has not independently developed.

## Role in Solasfall
The Runeweavers supply magical infrastructure across the city — runic wards, lighting systems, mana-conductive conduits, and the enchanted components embedded in civic structures. They collaborate with the Order of the Gilded Chain on runed armour and weapons, and with the Lifebinders on healing tools and expedition equipment.

Their magic tool production is the institution's most visible commercial function and a significant source of revenue. The complexity of a tool's second circle determines its value — simple tools are widely available, while tools encoding sophisticated spell structures are expensive and carefully controlled.

## Relationships with Other Institutions
Work closely with **Lifebinders** on equipment incorporating biological effects and with the **Order of the Gilded Chain** on military applications. Collaborate with **Riftconjurors** occasionally on circle structures for spatial applications.

Their relationship with **Elemental Conflux** is competitive in the tools market — Conflux mages sometimes produce their own functional tools, undercutting Runeweaver commercial interests.

## Culture & Reputation
The Runeweavers are regarded as the most methodical and artisan-oriented of the institutions. Citizens interact with their work constantly without always recognising it — the enchanted infrastructure of daily life in Solasfall is largely Runeweaver in origin. Among other mages they are respected for precision and occasionally condescended to for their lack of raw casting power.

Internally the culture fuses craftsmanship with engineering. Students undergo rigorous training in geometric precision, structural theory, and the physical construction of circles under pressure. The institution holds that a circle is only as reliable as its least precise line, and this philosophy shapes everything from teaching methods to quality control on commercial output.
