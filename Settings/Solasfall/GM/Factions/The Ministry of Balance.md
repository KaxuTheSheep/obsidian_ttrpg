### Overview
The Ministry of Balance is the unseen hand that controls Solasfall’s survival through cold, precise calculation. Established shortly after the creation of the Veil, this institution regulates the city’s economy, enforces ruthless taxation, and ensures the flow of magical energy required to maintain the delicate balance between life and death. Its authority derives not only from armies or sorcery but from its unyielding grip on resources and a doctrine demanding sacrifice without mercy.

### Purpose & History
Formed to stabilize Solasfall’s chaotic trade of Mana Stones, the Ministry introduced Gilded Mints — a currency magically pegged to the value of the stones harvested from the Hollowdeep. This replaced the unreliable physical weighing of mana and created a rigid economic order that drives exploration while punishing failure.

### Core Responsibilities
- **Pegging the Currency:**  
    The Ministry constantly recalculates the exchange rate between Mana Stones and Gilded Mints, artificially controlling scarcity to motivate citizens into perilous expeditions.
- **Taxation and Enforcement:**  
    Every citizen must pay their due in Gilded Mints during the dreaded Weighing event, on pain of death or forced conscription into Hollowdeep salvage teams.
- **The Weighing:**  
    Occurring two weeks after the Rift opens, this ritual audits the city’s wealth and punishes those who fail to maintain their share of the “balance.” It is both an economic and magical procedure, exploiting the waning Veil as justification for enforcement.
    

### Public Perception
To most, the Ministry is a heartless, inevitable force. Merchants grudgingly respect the stability it brings, while explorers and common folk alike fear its reach. Rumours circulate of secret vaults brimming with hoarded mana and forbidden knowledge, deepening suspicion and unease.

### Symbol & Motto
A set of scales forged from gold and stone, forever tipped unevenly to remind citizens of their precarious place in the grand equation.  
_"Balance is Survival."_
