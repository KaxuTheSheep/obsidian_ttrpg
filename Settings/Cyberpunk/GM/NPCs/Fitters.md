# Fitters

*The profession that puts hardware inside people. Licensed or not.*

---

## What a Fitter Is

A fitter is a medical and technical specialist who installs, removes, and maintains augmentations. The profession sits at the intersection of surgery and engineering — understanding both the biological tissue being interfaced with and the hardware being integrated into it.

The name is street vernacular that has become the universal term across all tiers of the profession. The formal title varies by tier and context but everyone from conglomerate clients to forgotten district residents uses the same word.

---

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1 — Conglomerate Fitters

Employed directly by conglomerates as part of their internal infrastructure. These are the most technically equipped fitters in New Halcyon — access to current hardware, sterile facilities, full diagnostic suites, and direct supply chain relationships with augment manufacturers.

They are not available to the general public. Access is through conglomerate employment, conglomerate adjacent relationships, or paying rates that effectively restrict them to the wealthy. Their work is legitimate in every formal sense the Federal States' limited regulatory framework recognises.

Conglomerate fitters operate under institutional oversight — their work reflects on the conglomerate's brand and they are held to internal standards accordingly.

### Tier 2 — Licensed Independent Fitters

Trained through formal education in augment installation and biological interfacing. In a functioning state this would make them equivalent to specialist surgeons. In the Federal States' unregulated environment it makes them qualified professionals operating without the institutional support that qualification would normally come with.

A tier 2 fitter has the knowledge. They may not have every tool, the most current equipment, or access to the full range of augment stock. They operate independently — their own premises, their own client relationships, their own reputation as their primary asset.

This is the tier most residents outside conglomerate structures interact with. A good independent fitter in a mid-tier district is a known quantity — people talk, recommendations travel, and a fitter's reputation is their business model.

### Tier 3 — Unlicensed Fitters

No formal education. Knowledge acquired through salvaged technical manuals, observation, apprenticeship under other unlicensed operators, and direct trial and error. They exist because the demand exists — forgotten districts have residents who need augmentation and cannot afford or access anything else.

Tier 3 fitters are not universally incompetent. Some have accumulated genuine skill through years of practice. Others are dangerous. The problem is that without formal training or institutional accountability there is no reliable way to distinguish between them before the procedure. Reputation matters here more than anywhere — in tight communities, a fitter who has harmed clients does not stay in business long.

They work with what they can source — salvage, grey market stock, components of uncertain provenance. Their installations reflect their tools and their training.

## Education and Entry

**Tier 1** entry is through conglomerate recruitment — typically identifying candidates from tier 2 backgrounds or occasionally funding education directly in exchange for employment contracts.

**Tier 2** education exists in the Federal States as a genuine technical discipline. Schools and programmes vary in quality given the absence of standardised accreditation, but the knowledge base is real and the training is substantive. Graduates enter a profession where their credentials open doors but do not guarantee the infrastructure to work properly.

**Tier 3** has no formal entry point. People become unlicensed fitters through necessity, proximity to the practice, or lack of other options. Some start as assistants to other unlicensed operators. Some learn from documentation never intended as training material. The variation in outcome reflects the variation in how they got there.

---

## Economic Reality

Fitters are also financiers. When a client wants an augment the fitter sources it, pays a down payment to the supplier on installation, and recoups through the client's payment plan. This means:

- A fitter's available stock is constrained by their cash flow and supplier relationships
- Tier 1 fitters with conglomerate backing can front expensive hardware tier 2 independents cannot
- Tier 2 independents have genuine constraints on what they can offer without advance notice or client deposit
- Tier 3 fitters work with what they can acquire rather than what they can order

A fitter who a client has defaulted on retains leverage — in some cases including remote access to hardware still inside the client's body. This is a last resort. Using it publicly damages reputation in communities where reputation is everything. It happens. It is not the norm.

---

## Social Position

Fitters occupy an interesting social position in New Halcyon — necessary to anyone who wants augmentation, trusted with access to the body in a city where that trust is not given lightly.

In conglomerate territory they are professionals within an institutional hierarchy. In mid-tier districts they are independent operators with genuine community standing. In forgotten districts the unlicensed fitter may be the closest thing to a medical practitioner the community has access to — handling augment work but also injuries, complications, and conditions that have nowhere else to go.

The relationship between a client and their fitter over time is not purely transactional. It accumulates history, trust, and obligation in both directions.
